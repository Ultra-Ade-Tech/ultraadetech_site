Optiver Foundation Scholarships

This scholarship programme will increase the number of women from low and middle-income countries who are able to take up offers to study science, technology, engineering and mathematics (STEM) subjects at postgraduate level at Oxford. The programme, which will provide support for up to 30 taught masterâ€™s students over a period of five years, has been made possible by a generous donation from the Optiver Foundation.

About

The next cohort of Optiver Foundation Scholars will commence their studies at Oxford in October 2025. These scholars will join the Departments of Statistics, Computer Science, Physics, Chemistry, Engineering Science, or the Mathematical Institute. They will receive full financial support, including course fees and a living stipend. Additionally, the scholarship includes tailored induction activities, mentoring, and ongoing support.

The Optiver Foundation's generosity will also enable Oxford to develop and pilot a new method for assessing the socio-economic background of international applicants. This will ensure that the scholarship funding is precisely targeted at those who need it most. The findings from this pilot will inform future scholarship programs at the University.

Eligibility

Oxford is dedicated to fostering the next generation of global researchers, academics, and scientific leaders. However, financial barriers often prevent talented students from pursuing postgraduate studies, especially those from disadvantaged backgrounds. In STEM fields, despite recent progress, additional barriers still result in lower participation of women in postgraduate courses, both internationally and at Oxford.

By focusing on women from low to middle-income countries, the Optiver Foundation Scholarship Programme will play a crucial role in increasing diversity in STEM subjects at Oxford and contributing to a more diverse workforce in the long term.
