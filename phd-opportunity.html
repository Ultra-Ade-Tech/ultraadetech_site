PhD Opportunity: Simulating Human Blood Flow on Exascale Computers

Develop HemeLB, a powerful code for simulating blood flow throughout the entire human body, for use on the world's most powerful supercomputers.

Learn More

Project

Develop HemeLB, a powerful code for simulating blood flow throughout the entire human body, for use on the world's most powerful supercomputers.

Highlights

  • Be involved in cutting-edge research influencing the design of future supercomputers.
  • Improve HemeLB for high-resolution simulations, visualization, and user interaction.
  • Contribute to the development of human digital twins for medical applications.

Details

  • 4-year fully funded PhD position at UCL (start 2025).
  • Work with a team known for interdisciplinary research and international collaborations.
  • Access to top-tier computing resources like Frontier and Isambard-AI.

You will

  • Become familiar with HemeLB code and adapt it for various platforms.
  • Develop features for user-friendly control and visualization of simulations.
  • Collaborate with NVIDIA for optimization on Isambard-AI.

If you are

  • Interested in computational biomedicine and supercomputing.
  • Self-motivated and enjoy working in a collaborative environment.

Contact

Professor Peter Coveney (p.v.coveney@ucl.ac.uk) with CV, transcript, and motivation letter.

Deadline

March 31, 2025 (rolling admissions)
