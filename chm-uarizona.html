UA Chemistry and Biochemistry PhD Funding

Students in the Chemistry and Biochemistry PhD program are supported for the duration of their studies at UA through research or teaching assistantships, as well as full graduate tuition remission and health insurance. All PhD students in good standing are provided financial support in the form of a teaching or research assistantship. In 2023, the stipend for these assistantships was $29,000.

Application Information:

Applications are accepted after August 1st each year. Fall Semester Admission: Application materials must be submitted before December 15th. Apply online using the UA Graduate Admissions App. No Application Fee for Fall 2025.

Application Requirements:

1. Three letters of recommendation. One letter must come from your research advisor. These letters should be submitted by your referees using the online system.

2. Transcripts from all colleges and universities attended or currently attending. Transcripts should be uploaded in your online application. A minimum GPA of 3.0 is typically required.

3. A brief Statement of Purpose, describing your past research experiences, goals, teaching experience, and why you want to attend The University of Arizona.

Fellowships and Awards:

Excellence in all areas of scholarly activity is valued. A large number of fellowships and awards are offered. Click here for a list.

Additional Information:

Many students have received highly-competitive fellowships, including prestigious National Science Foundation predoctoral fellowships, which support them for the duration of their studies in the program. Students engaged in interdisciplinary research through externally funded research programs may qualify for training grant support.
