A Helpful Grad School Application Timeline

Applying to graduate school can be a daunting task, but following a timeline can make the process more manageable. Starting early is key to ensuring that you meet all testing and application deadlines and make a potentially stressful experience easier. Here's a helpful grad school application timeline to guide you through the process:

May:
  • Start researching programs that fit your requirements.
  • Consider whether you can afford to go to school full time or whether a part-time degree program is better.
  • Think about the cost of living in the city where you would be.
  • Start taking practice tests for the GRE and any other necessary entrance tests.
June:
  • Sign up for a prep course for the GRE and any other required tests, and register to take them.
  • Request information from programs you’re interested in.
  • Start thinking about who to ask for recommendation letters. For some degree programs, both academic and professional recommendations are required.
July:
  • Request information from programs you’re interested in.
  • Start thinking about who to ask for recommendation letters. For some degree programs, both academic and professional recommendations are required.
August:
  • Take required tests.
  • Start drafting your statement of purpose. It may seem early, but the earlier you start, the stronger your application will be.
September:
  • Finalize the list of programs you are applying to.
  • Have one or more people review your statement of purpose.
October:
  • Request transcripts from your undergraduate school and from any other graduate work you have already done.
  • Take the GRE subject test, if needed.
  • Continue revising your statement of purpose to make sure it captures your unique story and interests.
November:
  • Continue revising your statement of purpose to make sure it captures your unique story and interests.
December:
  • Submit your applications.

Following this timeline will help make the grad school application process less stressful and more manageable. Starting early will not only help make sure you meet testing and application deadlines, but it will also make a potentially stressful experience a lot easier.
