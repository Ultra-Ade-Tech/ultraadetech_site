PhD Fellowship in Intra-body Semantic Communications – Theory and Principles for Receiver Design

Norwegian University of Science and Technology (NTNU)

Scholarship Details

The Norwegian University of Science and Technology (NTNU) offers a PhD Fellowship in Intra-body Semantic Communications – Theory and Principles for Receiver Design. This project focuses on developing a theoretical framework for biocommunication using semantic communication theory and principles to detect bacterial infections inside the body.

NTNU is committed to diversity and inclusion. Trondheim is a modern European city with a high quality of life. More information about NTNU and the application process can be found in the job advertisement.

Job Title:

PhD Fellowship in Intra-body Semantic Communications – Theory and Principles for Receiver Design

Department:

Department of Electronic Systems

Location:

Trondheim, Norway

Duration:

3 years

Deadline:

October 1st, 2024

Project Focus:

Developing a theoretical framework for biocommunication using semantic communication theory and principles to detect bacterial infections inside the body.

Responsibilities:

  • Developing novel theories based on semantic communications with a proposal for receiver design.
  • Validating simulation results with in-vitro experiments.

Qualifications:

  • Master's degree (or equivalent) in Wireless Communication, Electrical Engineering with course credits in information theory, or a similar field.
  • Strong academic background with a grade of B or better (NTNU grading scale).
  • Good written and spoken English language skills (TOEFL, IELTS, Cambridge Certificate test scores required if not a Scandinavian language speaker).
  • Self-motivated, independent, and team player with strong communication skills.

Benefits:

  • Competitive salary (starting at NOK 532,200 per year) with benefits like pension scheme and employee benefits.
  • Exciting and stimulating work environment in a strong international research setting.
  • Opportunity to contribute to a groundbreaking project with potential healthcare applications.

How to Apply:

  • Submit your application electronically via jobbnorge.no with your CV, diplomas, and certificates attached.
  • Include a research plan or project proposal (max 3 pages).
  • Include academic publications (up to 10 works) you want considered.
  • Provide contact information for three referees.

Additional Information:
