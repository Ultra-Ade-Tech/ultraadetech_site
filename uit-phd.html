PhD Fellowship in Optics: Quantitative Phase Microscopy and Tomography

UiT The Arctic University of Norway

Scholarship Details

UiT The Arctic University of Norway offers a PhD Fellowship in Optics: Quantitative Phase Microscopy and Tomography. This project involves the development of a groundbreaking quantitative phase microscopy and tomography platform to tackle multiple scattering in biological specimens.

UiT The Arctic University of Norway is committed to diversity and inclusion. Admission to the PhD program is required for employment. See the full job description for detailed information about the research project, qualifications, and application process.

Project:

Development of a groundbreaking quantitative phase microscopy and tomography platform to tackle multiple scattering in biological specimens.

Department:

Department of Physics and Technology, Ultrasound, Microwaves and Optics research group (UMO)

Location:

Tromsø, Norway

Duration:

4 years (75% funded by RCN research project, 25% for teaching)

Deadline:

October 20th, 2024

Who should apply?

  • Master's degree (or equivalent) in Physics, Optics/Optical Engineering/Computational Optics
  • Interest in bioimaging
  • Background in optics/optical engineering/photonics (experience with experimental setups a plus)
  • Experience in computational optics, optical tomographic reconstruction, and inverse scattering analysis algorithms
  • Experience in image analysis and optics simulations
  • Proficiency in English

Bonus points for:

  • Strong background in optical phase tomography setup and reconstruction algorithms
  • Previous experience with fundamental optics/photonics and computational optics
  • Independence, self-motivation, creativity, and excellent work ethic

Benefits:

  • Involvement in an exciting research project
  • Good career opportunities
  • Stimulating academic environment
  • Flexible work hours and competitive salary (NOK 532,200/year with annual increase)
  • Pension scheme and healthcare access
  • Opportunity to contribute to advancements in biological research

Application Requirements:

  • Cover letter explaining your motivation and research interests
  • CV
  • Diplomas and transcripts for bachelor's and master's degrees (including grading system explanation for foreign education)
  • Proof of English proficiency
  • References with contact information
  • Master's thesis (or draft if near completion) and other academic works
