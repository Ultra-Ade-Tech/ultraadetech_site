PhD Positions - University of Copenhagen

The University of Copenhagen is offering multiple PhD fellowships in various fields, providing exciting opportunities for aspiring researchers. These fellowships are designed to foster innovative research and academic excellence across diverse disciplines.

  • PhD in Quantum Physics and Atomic Physics
  • PhD in Extended Reality for Behaviour Change
  • PhD fellowship in Food Science
  • PhD stipends in Mathematics
  • Three PhD fellowships in Machine Learning

1. PhD in Quantum Physics and Atomic Physics

The Niels Bohr Institute at the University of Copenhagen is offering a PhD fellowship in Quantum Physics and Atomic Physics, focusing on developing a Cavity-based platform with neutral atoms for quantum simulations. The project is part of the Novo Nordisk Foundation Quantum Computing Programme (NQCP) and aims to establish a cutting-edge quantum programme in Copenhagen.

  • Start Date: February 1, 2025, or as soon as possible thereafter.
  • Project: Investigations of Cavity-mediated atomic interactions using ultra-cold neutral strontium atoms to simulate various analogue models.
  • Responsibilities: Construct experimental systems, develop interaction schemes, realize physical models, and work on numerical simulations and physical interpretation.
  • Qualifications: Background in Physics, Nano-Science, or Electrical Engineering; experimental/computational competences in quantum physics; academic drive and experimental ingenuity.
  • Program Options:
    • Option A: Three-year full-time study for those with a relevant Danish master’s degree.
    • Option B: Up to five-year full-time study for those with a relevant Danish bachelor’s degree.
  • Application Deadline: November 1, 2024.
  • Application Requirements: Motivated letter, CV, diplomas, transcripts, publication list, reference letters.
  • Supervisors: Professor Jan W. Thomsen; secondary supervisor: Assistant Professor Stefan Alaric Schäffer.
  • Application Process:
    • Submit application electronically.
    • After the deadline, selected applicants will be assessed by an expert assessor.
    • Assessed applicants will have the opportunity to comment on their assessment.
  • Contact Information: For specific information about the PhD fellowship, contact the supervisor. General information about PhD study at the Faculty of SCIENCE is available on the PhD School’s website.

2. PhD in Extended Reality for Behaviour Change

The Pioneer Centre for AI and the Department of Computer Science at the University of Copenhagen invite applications for a fully funded PhD Fellowship, working with Assistant Professor Teresa Hirzle and Professor Kasper Hornbæk. The starting date is May 1, 2025, or later.

  • Project: Explore the potential of XR for behavior change. Virtual Reality can help train responses to various situations (e.g., exposure therapy) and allows us to experience the consequences of our actions that are otherwise difficult to anticipate. Augmented Reality (AR) integrates information into our daily lives, making it practical for everyday use. The project aims to understand the underlying factors that drive behavior change in XR, focusing on fundamental XR properties like embodiment and presence. It also aims to develop a framework for interventions that integrate behavior change techniques into everyday life using XR technology, supporting sustained change.
  • Responsibilities: Shape the focus of the work and research questions. The project will use methods from human-computer interaction, including implementing and empirically evaluating XR prototypes with quantitative and qualitative methods. Knowledge and interest in human-computer interaction and extended reality are required.
  • Qualifications:
    • Master (Option A) or Bachelor (Option B) degree in Computer Science, Human-Computer Interaction, or a related field.
    • A curious mindset with a strong interest in designing technologies and technology-driven behavior change.
    • Comfort with software development for VR/AR (e.g., C# and Unity3D or Unreal Engine) and analysis/scripting.
    • Experience with user interface design and/or designing and performing user studies is helpful.
    • Strong team working and critical thinking skills.
    • Strong English language skills.
    • Relevant publications, if any.
  • Program Options:
    • Option A: A three-year full-time study within the framework of the regular PhD programme (5+3 scheme), if you already have an education equivalent to a relevant Danish master’s degree.
    • Option B: An up to five-year full-time study programme within the framework of the integrated MSc and PhD programme (the 3+5 scheme), if you do not have an education equivalent to a relevant Danish master’s degree but have an education equivalent to a Danish bachelor’s degree.
  • Application Deadline: December 1, 2024.
  • Application Requirements: Motivated letter of application (max. one page), CV, diplomas, transcripts, publication list, reference letters.
  • Application Process:
    • Submit application electronically.
    • After the deadline, a number of applicants will be selected for academic assessment by an unbiased expert assessor.
    • Interviews with selected candidates are expected to be held toward the end of April/early May 2025.
  • Contact Information: For specific information about the PhD fellowship, please contact the principal supervisor. General information about PhD study at the Faculty of SCIENCE is available at the PhD School’s website.

3. PhD Fellowship in Food Science

The Department of Food Science, Section of Ingredient and Dairy Technology at the University of Copenhagen, invites applicants for a PhD fellowship in Food Ingredients Functionality Experimentation. The project is part of the "AI4NaturalFood" research project, financed by the Novo Nordisk Foundation. The expected start date is January 1, 2025, or as soon as possible thereafter.

  • Project: Develop efficient experimentation methods to characterize the functionality of complex, mildly processed plant-based food ingredients in end products.
  • Qualifications:
    • Backgrounds in food product or food processing engineering, chemical engineering, or mechanical engineering.
    • Professional qualifications relevant to the PhD project.
    • Relevant publications.
    • Relevant work experience.
    • Other relevant professional activities.
    • Curious mindset with a strong interest in exploring new experimental methods.
    • Good language skills.
  • Application Deadline: October 25, 2024.
  • Application Requirements: Motivated letter of application (max. one page), CV, diplomas, transcripts, publication list, reference letters.
  • Application Process:
    • Submit application electronically.
    • After the deadline, a number of applicants will be selected for academic assessment by an unbiased expert assessor.
    • Interviews with selected candidates are expected to be held in week 44.
  • Contact Information: For specific information about the PhD fellowship, please contact the principal supervisor. General information about PhD study at the Faculty of SCIENCE is available at the PhD School’s website.

4. PhD Stipends in Mathematics

The Department of Mathematical Sciences (MATH) at the University of Copenhagen invites applicants for PhD stipends in mathematics. The start date is ordinarily September 1, 2025, but can be negotiated.

  • Available Stipends:
    • Algebra & Geometry Section and Copenhagen Center for Geometry & Topology (GeoTop):
      • Algebraic and Arithmetic Geometry
      • Applied Algebra
      • Combinatorics
      • Geometric Group Theory
      • Geometry and Geometric Analysis
      • Number Theory
      • Representation Theory
      • Topology
    • Analysis and Quantum Section, Centre for the Mathematics of Quantum Theory, and Quantum for Life Center:
      • Classical Analysis
      • Descriptive Set Theory
      • Functional Analysis
      • Geometric Group Theory
      • Harmonic Analysis
      • History of Mathematics
      • Mathematical Physics
      • Operator Algebras
      • Partial Differential Equations
      • Quantum Information
      • Quantum Computation and Quantum Simulation
  • Qualifications Needed for the Regular Programme: To be eligible for the regular PhD programme, you must have completed a degree programme equivalent to a Danish master’s degree (180 ECTS/3 FTE BSc + 120 ECTS/2 FTE MSc) related to the subject area of the project, e.g., Mathematics.
  • Terms of Employment in the Regular Programme: Employment as a PhD fellow is full time and for a maximum of 3 years. Employment is conditional upon your successful enrolment as a PhD student at the PhD School at the Faculty of SCIENCE, University of Copenhagen. The terms of employment and salary are in accordance with the agreement between the Danish Ministry of Taxation and The Danish Confederation of Professional Associations on Academics in the State.
  • Qualifications Needed for the Integrated MSc and PhD Programme: If you do not have an education equivalent to a relevant Danish master’s degree, you might be qualified for the integrated MSc and PhD programme if you have an education equivalent to a relevant Danish bachelor’s degree.
  • Terms of the Integrated Programme: To be eligible for the integrated scholarship, you are (or are eligible to be) enrolled in one of the faculty’s master programmes in Mathematics or Quantum Information Science. Students on the integrated programme will enroll as PhD students simultaneously with completing their enrollment in this MSc degree programme. The duration of the integrated programme is up to five years and depends on the amount of credits that you have passed on your MSc programme. Until the MSc degree is obtained (when exactly two years of the full 3+5 programme remains), the grant will be paid partly in the form of 48 state education grant portions (in Danish: “SU-klip”) plus salary for work (teaching, supervision, etc.) totaling a workload of at least 150 working hours per year. A PhD grant portion is currently (2024) DKK 6,820 before tax.
  • Responsibilities and Tasks in Both PhD Programmes:
    • Complete and pass the MSc education in accordance with the curriculum of the MSc programme (ONLY when you are attending the integrated MSc and PhD programme).
    • Carry through an independent research project under supervision.
    • Complete PhD courses corresponding to approx. 30 ECTS / ½ FTE.
    • Participate in active research environments, including a stay at another research institution, preferably abroad.
    • Teaching and knowledge dissemination activities.
    • Write scientific papers aimed at high-impact journals.
    • Write and defend a PhD thesis on the basis of your project.
  • Qualifications:
    • Professional qualifications relevant to the PhD project.
    • Relevant work experience.
    • Other relevant professional activities.
    • Curious mindset with a strong interest in Mathematics.
    • Good language skills.
  • Application Deadline: November 15, 2024.
  • Application Requirements: Motivated letter of application (max. one page), CV, diplomas, transcripts.
  • Application Process:
    • Submit application electronically.
    • After the deadline, a number of applicants will be selected for academic assessment by an unbiased expert assessor.
    • Interviews with selected candidates are expected to be held in weeks 2-13, 2025.
  • Contact Information: For specific information about the PhD fellowship, please contact the principal supervisor. General information about PhD study at the Faculty of SCIENCE is available at the PhD School’s website.

5. Three PhD Fellowships in Machine Learning

The Department of Computer Science at the University of Copenhagen invites applicants for three PhD fellowships in Machine Learning.

  • Projects:
    • Trustworthy Machine Learning: Focus: Robustness, privacy (Differential Privacy), and unlearning in Machine Learning. Requirements: Strong skills in mathematics (probability, statistics, linear algebra), machine learning, and programming. Supervisors: Amartya Sanyal (amsa@di.ku.dk) and Yevgeny Seldin.
    • Robust Online Learning: Focus: Impact and mitigation of adversarial threats in online learning, particularly in distributed settings. Requirements: Strong background in ML and optimization; knowledge of distributed algorithms and stochastic processes is a plus. Supervisors: Nirupam Gupta (nigu@di.ku.dk) and Yevgeny Seldin.
    • Sustainable Machine Learning: Focus: Resource-efficient ML methods and their impact on sustainability. Requirements: Strong skills in mathematics and ML, with a drive towards advancing UN sustainable development goals. Supervisors: Raghavendra Selvan (raghav@di.ku.dk) and Erik B. Dam.
  • Application Deadline: January 15, 2025.
  • Start Date: September 1, 2025.
  • Working Hours: Full time.
  • Department/Location: Department of Computer Science.
  • Qualifications:
    • Professional qualifications relevant to the PhD project.
    • Strong interest in the relevant topics.
    • Academic background in relevant topics.
    • Good English language skills.
    • (Optional) Relevant publications and work experience.
  • Application Requirements: Motivated letter of application clearly mentioning the project you are applying for (max. two pages), CV, diplomas, transcripts, publication list.
  • Application Process:
    • Submit application electronically.
    • After the deadline, a number of applicants will be selected for academic assessment by an unbiased expert assessor.
    • Interviews with selected candidates are expected to be held in mid-to-end February 2025.
  • Contact Information: For specific information about the PhD fellowship, please contact the relevant supervisors. General information about PhD study at the Faculty of SCIENCE is available at the PhD School’s website.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Academic qualification for admission, including relevant degrees and proficiency in English.
  • Demonstrated research potential and academic excellence.
  • Relevant experience and publications in the respective field.
  • Strong interest in the chosen research area and a commitment to academic and research excellence.

Value of the Scholarship

  • Full tuition fees and expenses for accommodation.
  • Comprehensive support for the duration of the PhD program.
  • Opportunities for professional development and networking.
  • Access to state-of-the-art research facilities and resources.

Application Process

The application process includes submitting a motivated letter of application, CV, diplomas, transcripts, publication list, and reference letters. The deadline for applications is November 1, 2024. For specific information about each PhD fellowship, please contact the respective supervisor.
