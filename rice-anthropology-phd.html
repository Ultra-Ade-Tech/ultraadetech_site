Rice University Anthropology Ph.D. Program

Focus on Social/Cultural Anthropology and Archaeology with full funding for 5 years.

Learn More

Program Overview

The Rice University Anthropology Ph.D. program focuses on Social/Cultural Anthropology and Archaeology. It emphasizes collaborative, individualized, and rigorous research, with faculty dedicated to blending social science and humanities traditions.

Research Areas

  • Ethnography
  • Social discourse
  • Cultural criticism
  • Medical anthropology
  • Complex societies
  • Settlement studies
  • Food production origins
  • African archaeology
  • Historical archaeology

Funding

  • Full Funding for 5 Years: Includes tuition remission, health insurance subsidy, and stipend
  • Continued Support: Tuition waiver through year 6, reduced tuition through year 8
  • External Funding: Students are encouraged to seek additional funding for research and writing

Application Requirements

  • Deadline: December 28th
  • Application Fee: $85 (waived for Texas and bordering state residents)
  • Transcripts: Official transcripts required
  • GRE: Not required for 2024 admissions
  • TOEFL: Required for non-native English speakers (minimum 600 paper-based or 90 internet-based)
  • Statement of Proposed Research: 2,000 words max, outlining research interests and alignment with faculty
  • Statement of Additional Information: 1,500 words max, discussing relevant background and experiences
  • Letters of Recommendation: 3 letters from professors or mentors
  • Writing Sample: Up to 8,000 words, showcasing academic writing ability

Apply

For detailed information and access to the application portal, please visit the Rice University Anthropology Department website.
