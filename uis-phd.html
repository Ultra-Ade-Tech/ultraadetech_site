PhD Fellowship in Carbon Capture and Utilization (CCU)

University of Stavanger

Scholarship Details

The University of Stavanger offers a PhD Fellowship in Carbon Capture and Utilization (CCU). This PhD position focuses on converting captured CO2 from the air into renewable methane (CH4) using green hydrogen (H2). You will address the technical challenges and techno-economic assessment of this proposed technology.

The University of Stavanger is committed to diversity and welcomes applications from all qualified candidates. Women are encouraged to apply, and if equally qualified, will be given priority. The university offers a stimulating and international work environment.

University:

University of Stavanger (UiS)

Faculty:

Science and Technology

Department:

Energy and Petroleum Engineering

Job Title:

PhD Fellow in Carbon Capture and Utilization (CCU)

Location:

Stavanger, Norway

Starting Date:

No later than February 1st, 2025

Duration:

3 years (fixed term)

Deadline:

September 19th, 2024

Scope:

Full-time

Salary:

NOK 532,200 gross per year (subject to change) with salary development based on seniority.

Benefits:

  • Membership in the Norwegian Public Service Pension Fund
  • Favorable gym and sports club membership
  • Relocation program
  • Language courses
  • Discounted public transport
  • Access to healthcare, pensions, parental leave, and competitive salary
  • Guaranteed and affordable nursery places

Qualifications:

  • Master's degree (3+2) in Chemical Engineering (preferably recent) or a relevant qualification suitable for doctoral studies.
  • A strong academic background.
  • Minimum grades equivalent to a B on your master's thesis and overall degree.
  • Experience in experimental catalysis and reactor engineering (preferred).
  • Motivation and research potential within the field of CCU.
  • Ability to work independently and collaboratively, be innovative, and handle a heavy workload.
  • Excellent written and oral English communication skills.

English Language Requirements:

International applicants must meet one of the following requirements:

  • TOEFL iBT (minimum score: 90)
  • IELTS (minimum score: 6.5)
  • Cambridge English: CAE or CPE
  • PTE Academic (minimum score: 62)

Exemptions:

  • Applicants with one year of completed university studies in Australia, Canada, Ireland, New Zealand, UK, or USA.
  • Applicants with a completed master's degree taught in English in an EU/EEA country.
  • Applicants exempt based on NOKUT's GSU list.

Application Process:

Please submit your application online through the "Apply for this job" link. Your application should include:

  • A cover letter stating your research interests and motivation for the position.
  • CV with a full summary of your education and experience.
  • References, certificates/diplomas, and other relevant documentation.
  • Diploma Supplement or similar and a confirmed conversion scale (if applicable).
  • Documentation of English language proficiency (if required).
  • Publications or other relevant research work (optional).

Contact Information:

For more information about the position and project description, contact:

  • Professor Zhixin Yu: zhixin.yu@uis.no

For information about the application procedure, contact:

  • Sr. HR Advisor Margot A. Treen: rekruttering@uis.no

Additional Notes:

The University of Stavanger is committed to diversity and welcomes applications from all qualified candidates. Women are encouraged to apply, and if equally qualified, will be given priority. The university offers a stimulating and international work environment.
