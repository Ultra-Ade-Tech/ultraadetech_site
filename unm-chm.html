Chemistry and Biochemistry PhD Funding

Acceptance into the graduate program is based on academic performance, research experience, motivation, and how well the applicant meets the needs of the department to fulfill our teaching and research missions. All aspects are considered. For example, academic excellence may compensate for a lack of research experience and vice versa.

The GRE is not required.

International students need to take an English language test with minimum scores of TOEFL 80, IELTS 6.5, or Duolingo 105. An English language test is not required for students from countries teaching in English.

There is no application fee for your first application to NMSU. If you apply to another program or decide to reapply after being declined, you may be asked to submit an application fee.

Unofficial transcripts are acceptable for the application. Official transcripts are only required upon admittance.

It is strongly recommended that you contact potential research advisors to ensure availability and compatible research interests, but it is not required.

The Department does not offer an advanced degree in Biochemistry, only Chemistry. However, your thesis research can be conducted with a biochemistry faculty member and biochemistry is included in the course curriculum.

All graduate students are supported by either a teaching or research assistantship for the duration of their studies. The annual assistantship salary for a first-year student for the 2024-2025 academic year is $31,451. The salary increases as you make progress toward your degree.

Yes. The rights and responsibilities of graduate students are covered by a collective bargaining agreement.

Students holding a B.S. in Chemistry, Biochemistry, or a related degree are eligible to apply for either M.S. or Ph.D. degrees.

If you don't find the answers here, please contact the Graduate School at gradinfo@nmsu.edu.

Application Details:

There is no fee to apply!

International Graduate Program Applicants

Required Documents:

  • Unofficial transcripts from each institution previously attended (official transcripts required upon admission)
  • Statement of Purpose
  • Curriculum vitae
  • 3 letters of reference
  • TOEFL, IELTS, or Duolingo test scores*

*Score Requirements - Students from universities taught in English in these countries are exempt.

Deadlines

  • Application Deadline: February 15 for fall admission, July 15 for spring admission.
