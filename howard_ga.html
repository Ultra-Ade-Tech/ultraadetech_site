Ph.D. Research Assistant

Howard University

Ph.D. Research Assistant

Howard University is seeking multiple Ph.D. Research Assistants for the Department of Mechanical Engineering. The positions are based in Washington, D.C., and offer an exciting opportunity to work on cutting-edge research projects in advanced manufacturing.

About Howard University:

Established in 1867, Howard University stands as a premier private institution renowned for its academic excellence. Situated in the heart of Washington, District of Columbia (DC), the capital city of the United States, its campus enjoys a picturesque setting amidst the vibrant urban landscape. DC, with its delightful climate and scenic beauty, serves as the political and cultural nucleus of the nation, boasting an array of humanities, historical, and academic resources, including esteemed national museums, solemn memorials, and iconic landmarks.

The Washington metropolitan area emerges as a hub of scientific innovation, housing the headquarters of prestigious institutions such as the National Science Foundation, the National Institutes of Health, and the National Institute of Standards and Technology. Howard University, in recent years, has earned distinction as a federal key support university, fostering extensive collaborations with industry leaders such as Google, Amazon, and Autodesk, etc. Notably, the university bestowed an honorary doctorate upon former President Obama in recognition of his outstanding contributions.

About the Advanced Manufacturing Laboratory:

The Advanced Manufacturing Laboratory at Howard University is dedicated to conducting cutting-edge research in Multiphysics to pioneer innovative advanced manufacturing processes. Originally established as the Design, Fabrication, and Metrology Laboratory by Dr. Dan Feng in August 2022 at Texas A&M University in College Station, Texas, it will relocate to Howard University in Washington, D.C. in August 2024.

Over the years, the laboratory has nurtured over 20 exceptional undergraduate, master's, and doctoral research talents. It has also cultivated long-standing partnerships and academic exchanges with numerous renowned universities and companies across various domains, offering students diverse avenues for future employment opportunities. Please visit the laboratory's homepage for more details: https://sites.google.com/view/feng-lab/

About the Director (Principal Investigator):

Professor Dan Feng, the Principal Investigator of the laboratory, obtained his bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees from Huazhong University of Science and Technology, University of Missouri, and Purdue University, respectively. He has amassed extensive experience in scientific research and teaching throughout his tenure at Purdue University and Texas A&M University. Notably, he was honored with the Outstanding Teaching Award from the College of Engineering at Purdue University.

Professor Feng's research expertise traverses a multitude of disciplines, encompassing mechanical vibration, 3D printing, roll-to-roll manufacturing, machine vision, fluid-structure interaction, thermomechanics, and nanocomposite synthesis. His pioneering work on Feng's hydrodynamics functions has yielded a closed-form semi-analytical solution for 2D fluid-coupled web vibration systems. These achievements have captured the attention of Applied Materials, the leading manufacturer of high-performance roll-to-roll coating systems, resulting in successful patent leasing agreements.

Recruitment Information:

We aim to recruit more than two Ph.D. students with the anticipated starting date to be January 10, 2025.

Research Areas: Multi-physics-based development of advanced manufacturing, design, and measurement processes.

Research Applications: Roll-to-roll manufacturing, 3D printing, flexible electronics, non-destructive testing, and 5G/6G communication devices.

  • Expertise in computer vision and image processing
  • Expertise in polymer and photoelastic
  • Expertise in artificial intelligence and machine learning
  • Expertise in heat transfer.
