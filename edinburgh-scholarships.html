Edinburgh Doctoral College Scholarships

The University of Edinburgh offers PhD scholarships for students starting their PhD research at the University.

Learn More

Award

Each scholarship covers the University tuition fee and a stipend equivalent to the UKRI stipend rate (for 2024/25 this is Â£19,237), lasting 3-4 years.

The University of Edinburgh is committed to widening access to postgraduate study for underrepresented groups, including BAME students and those from disadvantaged communities.

Eligibility

  • Open to UK and overseas students starting their first year of on-campus PhD research in 2025-2026.
  • Applicants must have applied for a full-time or part-time PhD program.
  • Must have or expect a UK first class or 2:1 honours degree or equivalent.

Criteria

  • Outstanding academic merit and research potential.
  • Other factors such as financial status, nationality, and proposed field of study are not taken into account.
  • Candidates who have already obtained a PhD are not generally considered.

Training

Undertake a package of training and development to develop skills in areas such as teaching, public engagement, entrepreneurship, data science, and research.

Selection Procedure

The University will use the information provided on the scholarship application form together with supporting documentation to administer and assess applications.

Shortlisted candidates should expect to be invited to attend an interview which will take place either in person or by telephone.

Notifications

All applicants will be notified of the outcome by the relevant school or deanery. For further information, please contact the school or deanery directly.
