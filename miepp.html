Master in International Economics and Public Policy

The Master in International Economics and Public Policy (MIEPP) at Johannes Gutenberg University Mainz is designed to equip students with the skills and knowledge needed to excel in the fields of economics and public policy. The program offers a comprehensive curriculum that integrates theoretical knowledge with practical applications.

Tuition Fees:

There are no tuition fees at Johannes Gutenberg University Mainz. Students only have to pay an administrative fee for registration with the universityâ€™s student service. Costs are around 350 Euros per semester at the moment. This registration entitles students to different services such as public transportation, canteen, cafeterias, dormitories, and social counseling.

Admission Requirements:

All students have to go through a formal application procedure, by which the most talented participants will be selected for our program. The application process for studying at Johannes Gutenberg University Mainz depends on whether you reached your bachelor-level degree in Germany or not.

Prerequisites for admission are:

  • A completed bachelor-level degree in business, economics, or a related field of study with at least six semesters regular study period.
  • If you have not completed your degree yet, you can still be admitted if â€“ by the time of application â€“ you have already acquired at least 135 ECTS in a bachelorâ€™s program that takes six semesters and awards a total of 180 ECTS according to the European credit point system.
  • English Proficiency: Sufficient knowledge of the English language has to be documented by one of the following and should not be older than three years relative to the application deadline.

The required B2 English level can be proven by one of the following tests:

  • TOEFL test with at least 72 points (internet-based test, IBT), 47 points (revised TOEFL Paper-delivered Test, PdT)
  • IELTS (International English Language Testing System) with a minimum of 5.5 points
  • Cambridge First Certificate in English (FCE) or a higher level of the University of Cambridge ESOL Examinations like advanced (CAE) or proficient (CPE)
  • Telc English B2
  • Oxford Test of English (OTE) with an average of at least 111 of 140 points
  • Academic Pearson Test of English (PTE) with a result of 59-75 points
  • University Entrance Qualification from an English-language institute

Additional Documents:

A letter of motivation (in English, max. 1 page)

Short CV (in English, max. 2 pages)

Students who did not receive their Bachelor-level degree at a German University also need to get approval to study at Johannes Gutenberg University (see step 1 of the application procedure)

If available (not required):

  • Test results of the Graduate Record Examination (GRE)
  • Evidence of any relevant professional training you have completed or any time you have spent working professionally in a relevant field
  • Evidence of any outstanding achievements, such as receiving special academic funding, awards or prizes, having work published, holding lectures etc.

Application Procedure:

Students who do not hold a bachelor-level degree from Germany:

Step 1: Recognition of foreign certificates and diplomas

Please note that this process can take up to 8 weeks and must be completed before the regular program application can be started. We advise you to start with step 1 at least 6 weeks before the start of the application period.

A detailed step-by-step instruction including a list of required documents for the recognition of foreign certificates and diplomas is provided by the International Admission Office:

https://www.studium.uni-mainz.de/en/your-application/applying-with-international-certificates/recognition/

Step 2: Program application

As soon as you have received your official validation certificate, you may apply online for the Masterâ€™s Program via the International Office.

After completing and submitting the online form, you will receive the completed application to print. You must then send this application to the Johannes Gutenberg University Mainz by mail (to the address listed on the printout).

The application will only be processed when the online application has been completed, printed, signed and received by the Student Office or International Office with all required documents before the deadline.

Please note that you do not need any proof of German language proficiency and that the additional documents listed under the Admission Requirements are required for the selection process undertaken by the faculty.
